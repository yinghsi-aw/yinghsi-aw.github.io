What is a Thesis Statement?

Intro

An expository thesis statement is the most important sentence in your essay. It usually sits at the end of your first paragraph. Think of it as a GPS for your reader. Since an expository essay is meant to explain a topic clearly, this single sentence tells the reader exactly what you are going to explain, without mixing in your personal opinions. A good thesis statement should never be a giant, boring list, nor should it be a simple fact like "Computers are helpful." Instead, it should show a clear focus. For example, you could say: "Using computers in schools changes how students research information and work together on projects." This tells the reader the exact path your essay will take.

Give it a try

📝 Please find the thesis statement in the following paragraph and click on it 📝

In today’s world, smart devices are everywhere, and most students carry a smartphone in their pockets. While some teachers worry that phones distract teenagers during class, these devices can actually be great tools for study. Using smartphones in school improves learning by providing quick dictionary access and allowing students to participate in online class quizzes.

  • It is objective: It states a clear point without using weak, personal phrases like "I think" or "In my opinion."
  • It provides a clear roadmap: It precisely outlines the two subtopics that the rest of the essay will explain—quick dictionary access and online class quizzes. It tells the reader exactly what to expect.
